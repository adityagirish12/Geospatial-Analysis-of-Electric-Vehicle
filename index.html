STEP 1Coordinate Extraction & Filtering
Geospatial Prep
# Extract lat/lon from "POINT (-122.31 47.60)"
def parse_point(val):
    if pd.isna(val): return pd.Series([np.nan, np.nan])
    coords = val.replace('POINT (','').replace(')','').split()
    return pd.Series([float(coords[0]), float(coords[1])])

df[['Longitude','Latitude']] = df['Vehicle Location'].apply(parse_point)
df_wa = df.dropna(subset=['Latitude']).query("State == 'WA'").copy()
df_wa = df_wa.drop_duplicates(subset=['VIN (1-10)','DOL Vehicle ID'])
df_wa['EV_Type_Short'] = df_wa['Electric Vehicle Type'].map({
    'Battery Electric Vehicle (BEV)':'BEV',
    'Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicle (PHEV)':'PHEV'})
Result
279,671 valid coordinates (99.96%). After WA filter + dedup: 278,996 records, 20 columns, 0 duplicates. BEV: 223,279 | PHEV: 55,717.

ADV 01Animated County Growth (2015-2025)
Animated Mapbox · Cumulative
county_year = df_wa[df_wa['Model Year'].between(2015,2025)].groupby(
    ['County','Model Year']).agg(
    Count=('VIN (1-10)','count'),
    Lat=('Latitude','mean'), Lon=('Longitude','mean')).reset_index()

county_year_cum = []
for county in county_year['County'].unique():
    c = county_year[county_year['County']==county].sort_values('Model Year')
    c['Cumulative'] = c['Count'].cumsum()
    county_year_cum.append(c)
county_year_cum = pd.concat(county_year_cum)

fig11 = px.scatter_mapbox(county_year_cum,
    lat='Lat', lon='Lon', size='Cumulative',
    color='Cumulative', animation_frame='Model Year',
    hover_name='County', size_max=45, zoom=6,
    color_continuous_scale='Plasma')
Insight
Growth starts in King County, spreads to Snohomish, Pierce, Clark. Post-2020 acceleration dramatic. Eastern counties lag 3-4 years.

06 / Conclusion

Conclusion

This project analyzed 278,996 cleaned Washington State EV registration records across 39 counties and 47 manufacturers from data.gov. After extracting geospatial coordinates and standardizing categorical fields, we built 14 interactive Plotly visualizations including scatter maps, density heatmaps, animated temporal maps, treemaps, sunburst charts, and multi-dimensional scatter plots, all saved as HTML files. The analysis reveals King County holds 49.5% of registrations, Tesla dominates at 41% market share, BEVs outnumber PHEVs 4:1, and average BEV range has tripled from 75 to over 250 miles. Adoption is diffusing outward from urban cores, and Puget Sound Energy and Seattle City Light face the highest infrastructure demand. These insights support charging infrastructure planning, grid capacity management, and equitable transportation policy.
